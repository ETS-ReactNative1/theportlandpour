Commitment

You're passionate about what you do, and so are we. You put your heart and soul into your product, and now it's time to share it with the world. Don't worry, we got your back.

The Portland Pour is the only website dedicated to local craft distilling and craft cocktails in Oregon and the Pacific Northwest. Our passion for what we do is evident in our work, not just in our art, but in our writing, recipes, and reporting. We love Portland, and we love the beverages created here. Join us in our mission to bring the amazing products, places, and industry news of craft beverages in Portland to consumers, not just in Oregon, but around the world.

Collaboration

We're all about raising the profile of Portland craft distilling, and your brand. Sponsoring The Portland Pour means you not only value your local community and growing your brand, but you support the artists and writers working to keep the project entirely local, and free. We don't accept advertising, and rely on the support of our fellow Portland companies to keep our content fresh, on point, and active. We encourage you to consider a sponsorship package and collaborate with us.

Workshops and classes

We offer workshops and classes on topics ranging from "how to get started with local craft cocktails at home" to "making syrups, cordials, and bitters at home" to "studio lighting for product photography" and many others.

Sustaining sponsors have access to all of our workshops, either as participants or hosts. Want to host a workshop in your tasting room or bar? No problem. Just let us know when and where, and what topic you'd like to explore.

Creative services

The Portland Pour was designed to be a website, and a creative agency for companies big enough to have a marketing department, but not yet big enough to have a creative department in house. We are your creative department.

We provide all manner of creative services, including product photography, content creation, custom recipe writing, in house printing, print and digital design, and technical consulting for your website and digital marketing.

How to choose

Choosing a sponsorship level depends on your marketing goals, how you want to use The Portland Pour, and if you need any of our creative services. It also depends on your budget and overall return on the investment when compared to your existing marketing spend.

For those considering a sustaining sposorship, we recommend considering the sponsorship cost as a way to reduce the cost of both online promotion and creative support. How much do you spend for online advertising and does The Portland Pour fit with your target audience? How much do you spend on product photography, design, printing, or events? Is eleminating or reducing those costs valuable to your brand?

For many, it isn't. You may already have your creative covered, either in house or with existing vendors, and that's fine. We have a sponsorship level for you that includes our content, workshops, and all the other stuff, but saves significant cost by limiting the benefits of sponsorship to just the features you need.

Or, perhaps you're not in Portland. We only accept a limited number of sustaining sponsors each year, and we do require that they be in Portland or somewhere in the Pacific Northwest. If that's the case, we do apollogize, but would still love your support, and invite you to consider one of our other sponsorship levels.

How we choose

Community sponsorship is open to all, however, there are only a few sustaining sponsors. When we consider the level of service and care we offer our sustaining sponsors, we have to limit the number we accpet each year. Often, this is a difficult decision, and there are times when we get more requests than we can accommodate.

When selecting sustaining sponsors, we start with the order in which we received requests to become a sponsor, and the location of the potential sponsor. We also consider the appropriateness of the brand, and whether it will be a good fit for The Portland Pour. Ideally, we'll be able to accomodate a group of sustaining sponsors who's products compliment each other, and that give us a good selection of spirits and items that we can use in our art and recipes.

Startup sponsors are also restricted to beverage startups in Oregon. We know the overwhelming cost associated with a new business, and we want you to have the best start possible. However, we do ask that startups only avail themselves of this option once.
